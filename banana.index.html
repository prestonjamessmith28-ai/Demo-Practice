Where bananas came from

  • Southeast Asia is the birthplace of the banana.

  • The earliest wild bananas grew in regions that are now:
    • The Philippines
    • Malaysia
    • Indonesia
    • Papua New Guinea
  • Early bananas were not like today’s
  • People began domesticating wild banana species (mostly Musa acuminata and Musa balbisiana) thousands of years ago.

How bananas spread

From Southeast Asia, bananas were carried by early travelers to: India (long before 500 BC) Africa (brought by Austronesian sailors) Later to the Middle East and Mediterranean (spread via trade routes) Bananas arrived in the Americas only after the 1500s when Portuguese explorers brought them to the Caribbean.

Fun facts

  • The word “banana” probably comes from a West African language (possibly Wolof), meaning “finger.”
